Wellington Botanical Society


Te Ropu Manaki i Nga Tamariki a Tane,
o Te Whanganui a Tara

The Wellington Botanical Society encourages the study of botany, with a special focus on native New Zealand flora. Our members work to cultivate native plants and advocate for the protection of lands and waters in their natural state.

Meetings

Every month we get together to see guest speakers, hear about field trips, and chat about botanical topics.

Newsletters

We produce three newsletters a year. This is where you’ll find local botanical news, field reports, and everything else we do.

Join

The membership year for the Wellington BotSoc runs from 1 July to 30 June. New members are always welcome!

Identify Plants

Found an unusual plant that you’d like to identify? Send us a photo and we'll get our botanical experts to identify it for you.

Field Trips

We love observing native plants in their natural environment. That's why we hold day trips around the Wellington region.

More

Find out about publications, awards, Maori plant names, and more of what the Wellington BotSoc is involved in.
